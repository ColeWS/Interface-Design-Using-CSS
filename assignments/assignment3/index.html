Twix

What is a Twix?

Twix is a biscuit candy bar created by Mars Inc. The Twix comes in different forms, but the most common seen is with caramel and milk chocolate. The Twix comes packaged with one (snack size), two (standard size), or four bars (king size).

History of Twix

Twix was first produced in the United Kingdom in 1967, with it later coming out in the United States in 1976, and again in 1979. The name "Twix" is a blended word of "Twin Sticks". In mainland Europe, the Twix was actually called "Raider" before its name was changed in 1991 to match the name of the brand with the rest of the world.

Twix Products

Bars

  • The original candy bar was introduced in 1976 and reintroduced in 1979. It has the classic combination of a biscuit, milk chocolate, and caramel.
  • Twix Peanut Butter was introduced in 1983. It has the same as the original, just with peanut butter instead of caramel.
  • Twix Salted Caramel is exactly the same as the original just with salted caramel instead of regular caramel.
  • Twix Cookies and Creme was introduced in 1991. It has a chocolate biscuit with a creme and cookie bit filling taking place of the caramel.
  • Twix Ice Cream has vanilla ice cream that is coated in caramel and then also coated in milk chocolate.
  • Twix 100 Calories, as the name suggests, is a 100 calorie version of the original twix bar.
  • Twix Cookie Dough has a cookie dough filling instead of caramel.

Discontinued products

  • Twix Chocolate Fudge had a chocolate fudge filling instead of caramel and also had a chocolate biscuit.
